What is gPDF?

gPDF is an open-source, privacy-focused, and completely free suite of online PDF tools. We believe that your documents are your business, and we are committed to providing powerful, reliable tools without ever collecting your file data. Our platform runs entirely in your browser, ensuring that your files never leave your computer. With no ads, no registration, and no usage limits, gPDF offers a seamless and secure experience for all your PDF needs

Why use our Remove PDF Password tool?

  • Completely Free & Unlimited: Unlock as many PDFs as you need without any cost or restrictions.
  • Privacy First: Your files and passwords are processed directly in your browser. They are never uploaded to our servers, guaranteeing 100% privacy.
  • Open Source: Our code is publicly available, so you can verify our privacy claims and even contribute to the project.
  • Unlock Your PDFs: Regain access to password-protected PDFs for which you have the password.
  • No Registration Required: Use our tools instantly without the need to create an account or provide any personal information.
  • Fast and Efficient: Quickly decrypt your PDF and get an unlocked version in seconds.

How to use the Remove PDF Password tool:

  1. Step 1: Drag and drop your password-protected PDF file onto the upload area, or click to select it.
  2. Step 2: Enter the current password for the PDF in the options box.
  3. Step 3: Click the "Process Files" button to start the decryption.
  4. Step 4: Once processed, download your new, unlocked PDF from the results section.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is it legal to remove a password from a PDF?

You should only remove passwords from PDFs that you own or have the legal right to access. Using this tool to bypass security on documents you do not have permission to view may be illegal.

What if I don't know the password?

This tool requires the current password to decrypt the file. It cannot recover or crack a forgotten password. You must know the password to remove it.

Is my password or file content sent to your servers?

No. The entire decryption process happens locally in your browser. Your password and file data are never uploaded, ensuring your information remains completely private.
