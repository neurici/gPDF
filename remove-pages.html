What is gPDF?

gPDF is an open-source, privacy-focused, and completely free suite of online PDF tools. We believe that your documents are your business, and we are committed to providing powerful, reliable tools without ever collecting your file data. Our platform runs entirely in your browser, ensuring that your files never leave your computer. With no ads, no registration, and no usage limits, gPDF offers a seamless and secure experience for all your PDF needs.

Why use our Remove PDF Pages tool?

  • Completely Free & Unlimited: Remove pages from as many PDFs as you need without any cost or restrictions.
  • Privacy First: Your files are processed directly in your browser. They are never uploaded to our servers, guaranteeing 100% privacy.
  • Open Source: Our code is publicly available, so you can verify our privacy claims and even contribute to the project.
  • Precise Page Deletion: Easily specify individual pages or ranges to remove, giving you full control over your document.
  • No Registration Required: Use our tools instantly without the need to create an account or provide any personal information.
  • Fast and Efficient: Quickly remove unwanted pages and get your updated PDF in seconds.

How to use the Remove PDF Pages tool:

  1. Step 1: Drag and drop your PDF file onto the upload area, or click to select it from your device.
  2. Step 2: In the options box, enter the page numbers or ranges you want to remove (e.g., 2, 4, 6-9).
  3. Step 3: Click the "Process Files" button to start the page removal.
  4. Step 4: Once processed, download your new PDF with the specified pages removed from the results section.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is it safe to remove pages from my PDF here?

Absolutely. All processing happens in your browser, so your files never leave your device. We do not upload or store any of your documents, ensuring complete privacy.

How do I specify the pages I want to remove?

You can enter page numbers separated by commas (e.g., 2, 4, 10) for individual pages, or use a hyphen for a range of pages (e.g., 6-9). You can also combine them (e.g., 2, 4, 6-9).

Can I undo the page removal?

The process creates a new file with the pages removed. Your original file is not modified. If you make a mistake, you can simply process the original file again with the correct page numbers.
